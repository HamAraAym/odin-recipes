Chicken Shawarma

Description

Shawarma is a beloved Middle Eastern dish known for its flavorful, marinated meat, which can include lamb, chicken, beef, or turkey. The meat is seasoned with a blend of spices such as cumin, turmeric, paprika, garlic, and coriander, then slow-cooked on a vertical rotisserie. As the meat cooks, it is basted in its own juices, ensuring a succulent texture. The outer layers are shaved off and typically served in a warm flatbread like pita or laffa, along with fresh vegetables, pickles, and sauces such as tahini or garlic sauce.

This dish has gained worldwide popularity as a delicious and convenient street food. Its adaptability allows for various regional twists, making it a favorite in many countries. Shawarma can be enjoyed as a sandwich or wrap, or on a plate with rice or salad, offering a satisfying meal that combines rich flavors and diverse textures.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup malt vinegar
  • 1/4 cup plain yoghurt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 8 skinless boneless chicken thighs
  • 8 pita bread rounds

Steps

  1. Step 1: In a glass baking dish, mix together the malt vinegar, 1/4 cup yogurt, vegetable oil, mixed spice, cardamom, salt and pepper. Place the chicken thighs into the mixture and turn to coat. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  2. Step 2: Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). In a small bowl, mix together the tahini, 1/4 cup yogurt, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, and parsley. Season with salt and pepper, taste, and adjust flavors if desired. Cover and refrigerate.
  3. Step 3:Cover the chicken and bake in the marinade for 30 minutes, turning once. Uncover, and cook for an additional 5 to 10 minutes, or until chicken is browned and cooked through. Remove from the dish, and cut into slices.
